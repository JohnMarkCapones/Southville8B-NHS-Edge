## Welcome Parents and Guardians

### About This Guide

Welcome to the Southville 8B National High School digital portal! We're excited to provide you with this online platform that allows you to stay connected with your child's education. This guide is written specifically for parents and guardians to help you make the most of the parent portal.

### What You Can Do with the Parent Portal

The parent portal gives you 24/7 access to:

- **View Grades** - Check your child's current grades and academic progress
- **Monitor Attendance** - See attendance records and tardiness
- **Check Assignments** - View upcoming assignments and deadlines
- **Communicate with Teachers** - Send messages to your child's teachers
- **Read Announcements** - Stay updated on school news and events
- **View School Calendar** - See important dates and events
- **Access Report Cards** - Download and view report cards
- **Track Activities** - See your child's involvement in clubs and events

### Why Use the Parent Portal?

Research shows that parent involvement significantly improves student success. The parent portal makes it easier for you to:

- Stay informed about your child's academic performance
- Identify and address problems early
- Celebrate achievements and milestones
- Support your child's learning at home
- Communicate effectively with teachers
- Stay connected with the school community

### What You'll Need

To use the parent portal, you need:

- **Computer, Tablet, or Smartphone** - Any device with internet access
- **Internet Connection** - WiFi or mobile data
- **Parent Account** - Login credentials provided by the school
- **Email Address** - For receiving notifications and updates
- **Web Browser** - Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge (updated version)

Don't worry if you're not tech-savvy! This guide uses simple, step-by-step instructions with pictures to help you navigate the portal easily.

## Getting Started

### Creating Your Parent Account

#### How to Get Your Account

When your child enrolls at Southville 8B National High School, the school will create a parent portal account for you. You will receive your login information through one of these methods:

1. **Email** - Sent to the email address you provided during enrollment
2. **Text Message** - SMS with login credentials
3. **Printed Letter** - Given to your child to bring home
4. **In Person** - Provided by the school registrar

Your login credentials include:
- **Username** - Usually your email address or a unique ID
- **Temporary Password** - A password you'll change on first login

![Parent account welcome email](../images/parent-welcome-email.png)

#### First-Time Login

**Step 1: Go to the School Website**

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge)
2. Type in the school portal address: `https://southville8b.edu.ph`
3. You'll see the school homepage

![School homepage](../images/parent-homepage.png)

**Step 2: Click Login**

1. Look for the **"Login"** button at the top right corner
2. Click the **"Login"** button
3. The login page will appear

**Step 3: Enter Your Credentials**

1. Type your **username** (usually your email address)
2. Type the **temporary password** provided by the school
3. Click **"Sign In"**

![Login page](../images/parent-login-page.png)

**Step 4: Change Your Password**

For security, you must change your password on first login:

1. You'll be asked to create a new password
2. Enter a strong password:
   - At least 8 characters long
   - Mix of uppercase and lowercase letters
   - At least one number
   - At least one special character (!, @, #, $, etc.)
3. Type the same password again to confirm
4. Click **"Update Password"**

**Tips for Creating a Strong Password:**
- Use a phrase you'll remember: "MyChild@School2026!"
- Don't use obvious passwords like "password123"
- Don't use your child's name or birthday
- Write it down and keep it in a safe place
- Don't share your password with anyone

**Step 5: Complete Your Profile**

After changing your password, you'll be asked to complete your profile:

1. Verify your email address
2. Add your mobile number (for text notifications)
3. Upload a profile photo (optional)
4. Set your notification preferences
5. Click **"Save Profile"**

![Complete profile page](../images/parent-profile-setup.png)

**Step 6: You're All Set!**

Congratulations! You can now access the parent portal anytime. You'll be taken to your parent dashboard.

### Logging In (After First Time)

For future logins:

1. Go to `https://southville8b.edu.ph`
2. Click **"Login"**
3. Enter your username and password
4. Click **"Sign In"**

**Remember Me Option:**
- Check the "Remember Me" box to stay logged in on your personal device
- Don't use this on shared or public computers

### Forgot Your Password?

If you forget your password:

**Step 1: Click "Forgot Password"**

1. On the login page, click **"Forgot Password?"** link
2. Enter your email address
3. Click **"Reset Password"**

![Forgot password page](../images/parent-forgot-password.png)

**Step 2: Check Your Email**

1. Check your email inbox
2. Look for an email from Southville 8B NHS
3. Check your spam folder if you don't see it
4. Click the password reset link in the email

**Step 3: Create New Password**

1. The link will take you to a password reset page
2. Enter your new password
3. Confirm your new password
4. Click **"Reset Password"**

**Step 4: Log In**

1. Go back to the login page
2. Use your new password to sign in

**Still Having Trouble?**

Contact the school office:
- Phone: [School phone number]
- Email: info@southville8b.edu.ph
- In person: Visit the registrar's office

## Viewing Your Child's Academic Progress

### Viewing Grades

Understanding how your child is performing academically is one of the most important uses of the parent portal.

#### Accessing the Grades Section

**Step 1: Open Grades**

1. Click **"Grades"** in the main menu
2. You'll see your child's current grades

![Grades overview](../images/parent-grades-overview.png)

#### Understanding the Grades Page

The grades page shows all your child's subjects and current performance:

**Subject List:**

Each subject shows:
- **Subject Name** - e.g., Mathematics, English, Science
- **Teacher Name** - Subject teacher
- **Current Grade** - Current average in this subject
- **Grade Level** - Outstanding, Very Satisfactory, etc.
- **Last Updated** - When grade was last updated

**Color Coding:**

Grades are color-coded for easy understanding:
- **Dark Blue (90-100)** - Outstanding
- **Blue (85-89)** - Very Satisfactory
- **Light Blue (80-84)** - Satisfactory
- **Yellow (75-79)** - Fairly Satisfactory
- **Red (Below 75)** - Did Not Meet Expectations

#### Viewing Detailed Grades by Subject

**Step 1: Click on a Subject**

1. Click on any subject name to see details
2. A detailed view will open

**Step 2: View Grade Components**

For each subject, grades are divided into components:

**1. Written Works (30%)**
- Quizzes
- Tests
- Written exercises
- Shows individual scores and total

**2. Performance Tasks (50%)**
- Projects
- Laboratory work
- Presentations
- Performances
- Shows individual scores and total

**3. Quarterly Assessment (20%)**
- Major exam at end of quarter
- Comprehensive test of learning

**Grade Calculation Example:**

```
Subject: Mathematics 8

Written Works (30%):
  Quiz 1: 18/20 = 90%
  Quiz 2: 15/20 = 75%
  Quiz 3: 19/20 = 95%
  Average: 86.67% × 30% = 26.00

Performance Tasks (50%):
  Project 1: 45/50 = 90%
  Activity 1: 23/25 = 92%
  Average: 91% × 50% = 45.50

Quarterly Assessment (20%):
  Final Exam: 38/40 = 95%
  95% × 20% = 19.00

QUARTERLY GRADE: 26.00 + 45.50 + 19.00 = 90.50 (Outstanding)
```

![Detailed subject grades](../images/parent-subject-detail.png)

#### Understanding Each Component

**Written Works:**

Written works test your child's knowledge and understanding:
- Quizzes - Short tests on recent lessons
- Tests - Longer assessments covering multiple topics
- Essays - Written compositions
- Problem sets - Math or science problems

**What to look for:**
- Consistent scores or improvement over time
- Sudden drops that may need attention
- Areas where your child excels or struggles

**Performance Tasks:**

Performance tasks show how your child applies knowledge:
- Projects - Research or creative work
- Laboratory experiments - Hands-on science work
- Presentations - Oral reports or demonstrations
- Group work - Collaborative tasks
- Performances - For subjects like Music or PE

**What to look for:**
- Effort and creativity
- Understanding of concepts
- Ability to work independently or with others

**Quarterly Assessment:**

The quarterly exam tests overall learning for the grading period:
- Comprehensive exam at the end of each quarter
- Covers all topics studied in the quarter
- Important for final quarter grade

**What to look for:**
- Preparation and study habits
- Test-taking skills
- Overall mastery of quarter's content

**Selecting a Quarter:**

## Monitoring Attendance

Regular school attendance is crucial for academic success. The parent portal makes it easy to monitor your child's attendance.

### Viewing Attendance Records

#### Accessing Attendance

1. Click **"Attendance"** in the main menu

![Attendance overview](../images/parent-attendance-overview.png)

#### Understanding the Attendance Page

**Attendance Summary:**

At the top, you'll see:

- **Total School Days** - Days of instruction so far
- **Days Present** - Days your child attended

A calendar view shows:

![Attendance calendar](../images/parent-attendance-calendar.png)

### Types of Attendance

#### Present

Your child attended school:
- Arrived on time
- Stayed full day
- No issues

#### Tardy

Your child arrived late:
- **Time In** - Shows what time they arrived
- **Minutes Late** - How late they were
- School policy: [Number] tardies = 1 absence

**What to Do:**
- Ensure your child leaves home earlier
- Address morning routine issues
- Communicate challenges to school if ongoing

#### Absent

Your child missed school:

**Types of Absences:**

1. **Excused Absence**
   - Illness with medical certificate
   - Family emergency
   - Approved family travel
   - School-sanctioned activities
   - Religious observance

2. **Unexcused Absence**
   - No valid reason provided
   - Parent permission not obtained
   - Documentation not submitted

**Important:** Too many absences (excused or unexcused) can affect academic performance.

#### Excused vs. Unexcused

**Excused Absence:**
- Valid reason provided
- Proper documentation submitted
- Your child can make up missed work
- Doesn't count against attendance rate as heavily

**Unexcused Absence:**
- No valid reason or no documentation
- May result in disciplinary action
- May not be allowed to make up work
- Affects attendance rate more seriously

### Attendance Impact on Academic Performance

#### Understanding the Connection

Research shows strong correlation between attendance and grades:

- **95%+ Attendance** - Optimal for academic success
- **90-94% Attendance** - Moderate risk to learning
- **Below 90% Attendance** - High risk of academic difficulties

**View Attendance Impact:**

1. Go to **Attendance** > **Attendance Impact**
2. See correlation between attendance and grades
3. Chart shows:
   - Attendance rate
   - Academic performance
   - Subjects most affected by absences

![Attendance impact chart](../images/parent-attendance-impact.png)

#### Chronic Absenteeism

**What is Chronic Absenteeism?**

Missing 10% or more of school days (approximately 18+ days per year):

- Puts student at academic risk
- May trigger school intervention
- Can lead to retention (repeating grade)
- Affects social development

**If Your Child is Chronically Absent:**

The school may:
1. Schedule meeting with parents
2. Develop attendance improvement plan
3. Provide support services
4. Refer to counseling if needed
5. Work with family to address barriers

**Support Available:**

- School counselor
- Social worker
- Transportation assistance
- Health services
- Flexible scheduling (in some cases)

### Improving Attendance

#### Tips for Better Attendance

**1. Establish Routine:**
- Consistent bedtime and wake-up time
- Prepare the night before (clothes, bag, lunch)
- Allow enough time for morning routine
- Build in buffer time

**2. Prevent Illness:**
- Ensure adequate sleep
- Nutritious meals
- Regular handwashing
- Keep vaccinations current
- Stay home when genuinely sick

**3. Make School a Priority:**
- Schedule appointments after school when possible
- Plan family trips during school breaks
- Talk positively about school
- Address school avoidance issues early

**4. Communicate with School:**
- Inform school of ongoing health issues
- Discuss transportation problems
- Report bullying or social issues
- Work with school to solve barriers

**5. Track and Discuss:**
- Review attendance weekly with your child
- Celebrate good attendance
- Address patterns of absences
- Set attendance goals together

## Understanding the Grading System

### The K-12 Grading System

The Philippines follows the Department of Education (DepEd) K-12 grading system.

#### Grade Scale

**For Junior High School (Grades 7-10):**

| Numerical Grade | Descriptive Rating | Meaning |
|-----------------|-------------------|---------|
| 90-100 | Outstanding | Exceeds expectations |
| 85-89 | Very Satisfactory | Meets expectations well |
| 80-84 | Satisfactory | Meets expectations |
| 75-79 | Fairly Satisfactory | Minimally meets expectations |
| Below 75 | Did Not Meet Expectations | Needs improvement |

**For Senior High School (Grades 11-12):**

Some schools use transmuted grades:

| Percentage | Transmuted | Descriptor |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| 98-100 | 1.00 | Excellent |
| 95-97 | 1.25 | Excellent |
| 92-94 | 1.50 | Very Good |
| 89-91 | 1.75 | Very Good |
| 86-88 | 2.00 | Good |
| 83-85 | 2.25 | Good |
| 80-82 | 2.50 | Fair |
| 77-79 | 2.75 | Fair |
| 75-76 | 3.00 | Passing |
| Below 75 | 5.00 | Failed |

#### Passing Grade

- **Passing Grade:** 75 and above
- **Failing Grade:** Below 75
- Students must earn at least 75 in all subjects to pass

### Grade Components

Each subject grade is computed from three components:

#### Written Works (30%)

Tests your child's knowledge:
- Quizzes
- Unit tests
- Written exercises
- Problem sets

**Weight:** 30% of quarterly grade

#### Performance Tasks (50%)

Tests ability to apply knowledge:
- Projects
- Laboratory work
- Presentations
- Portfolios
- Performances

**Weight:** 50% of quarterly grade

#### Quarterly Assessment (20%)

Comprehensive exam at end of quarter:
- Covers all topics for the quarter
- Major examination
- Determines quarter grade

**Weight:** 20% of quarterly grade

### Computing Quarterly Grades

**Formula:**

```
Quarterly Grade = (WW × 30%) + (PT × 50%) + (QA × 20%)

Where:
WW = Written Works Average
PT = Performance Tasks Average
QA = Quarterly Assessment Score
```

**Example Computation:**

```
Mathematics - 1st Quarter

Written Works:
Quiz 1: 18/20 = 90%
Quiz 2: 17/20 = 85%
Quiz 3: 19/20 = 95%
Average: (90 + 85 + 95) / 3 = 90%

Performance Tasks:
Project: 45/50 = 90%
Problem Set 1: 24/25 = 96%
Problem Set 2: 22/25 = 88%
Average: (90 + 96 + 88) / 3 = 91.33%

Quarterly Exam:
Score: 38/40 = 95%

Quarterly Grade Computation:
(90 × 0.30) + (91.33 × 0.50) + (95 × 0.20)
= 27 + 45.67 + 19
= 91.67
Rounded to 92 (Outstanding)
```

### Computing Final Grades

#### Quarterly Grades

Four quarters per school year:
- 1st Quarter (June-August)
- 2nd Quarter (September-October)
- 3rd Quarter (November-January)
- 4th Quarter (February-April)

#### Final Grade

**Formula:**

```
Final Grade = (Q1 + Q2 + Q3 + Q4) / 4
```

**Example:**

```
Mathematics Quarterly Grades:
1st Quarter: 92
2nd Quarter: 88
3rd Quarter: 90
4th Quarter: 93

Final Grade: (92 + 88 + 90 + 93) / 4 = 90.75
Rounded to 91 (Outstanding)
```

#### General Weighted Average (GWA)

**GWA** is the average of all subject final grades:

```
GWA = Sum of all subject final grades / Number of subjects
```

**Example:**

```
Subject Final Grades:
Mathematics: 91
English: 88
Science: 89
Filipino: 90
Araling Panlipunan: 87
MAPEH: 92
TLE: 90
Values Education: 93

GWA = (91+88+89+90+87+92+90+93) / 8 = 90

General Weighted Average: 90 (Outstanding)
```

## Supporting Your Child's Learning

### Creating a Supportive Home Environment

#### Study Space

**Set Up Study Area:**

- Quiet location away from distractions
- Good lighting
- Comfortable chair and desk
- Organized supplies (pens, paper, books)
- Free from TV, games, excessive noise
- Computer/internet access if needed

**Tips:**

- Same location each day builds routine
- Keep space clutter-free
- Ensure good ventilation
- Have water available
- Display calendar with deadlines

#### Study Routine

**Establish Schedule:**

1. **Consistent Time**
   - Same time each day
   - Right after school or after dinner
   - When child is most alert

2. **Break Large Tasks**
   - 25-30 minutes of focused work
   - 5-10 minute breaks
   - Alternate subjects

3. **Prioritize**
   - Start with hardest subject
   - Or start with due dates approaching
   - Save easier tasks for when tired

4. **Review**
   - End with review of next day's classes
   - Pack bag for tomorrow
   - Check portal for new assignments

**Sample Schedule:**

```
4:00 PM - Arrive home, snack
4:30 PM - Homework (hardest subject)
5:00 PM - Break
5:10 PM - Homework (next subject)
5:40 PM - Break
5:50 PM - Finish remaining homework
6:30 PM - Review for tomorrow
7:00 PM - Dinner
8:00 PM - Free time
9:00 PM - Prepare for bed
9:30 PM - Bedtime
```

**Every Week:**

**Do's:**

✅ Be available for questions

### Encouraging Good Study Habits

#### Time Management

**Teach Your Child To:**

- Use a planner or calendar
- Break large assignments into steps
- Start early, not last minute
- Estimate time needed
- Build in buffer time

**Use Portal Tools:**

- Check assignment due dates

- Color-code by subject

**Help Your Child:**

- Keep binder or folders organized

**Good Study Strategies:**

1. **Start Early**
   - Don't cram the night before
   - Study a little each day
   - Review notes regularly

2. **Active Study**
   - Don't just reread
   - Practice problems
   - Make flashcards
   - Teach concepts to someone else
   - Create study guides

3. **Use Multiple Methods**
   - Visual (diagrams, charts)
   - Auditory (read aloud, videos)

### When to Seek Additional Help

#### Academic Struggles

**Consider Tutoring If:**

- Grades consistently below expectations

- Falling behind despite effort

- Peer tutoring (often free at school)
- Private tutor
- Online tutoring
- Study groups

- Chronic difficulty reading or math
- Much more struggle than peers

- Previous retention

**What to Do:**

1. Discuss concerns with teacher
2. Request school counselor consultation
3. Consider learning assessment
4. Explore special education services if needed
5. Get vision/hearing checked

- Learning difficulties are common

- Many successful people have learning differences
- Support and accommodations available

#### Emotional or Social Issues

**Signs to Watch:**

- Doesn't want to go to school
- Complains of stomachaches/headaches
- Mood changes
- Change in friend group
- Anxiety about school
- Sleep problems

**Steps:**

1. Talk with your child
2. Contact teacher for their observations
3. Meet with school counselor
4. Consider professional counseling if serious
5. Work with school on support plan

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Account and Access

**Q: I forgot my password. How do I reset it?**

A: On the login page, click "Forgot Password?" Enter your email address, and you'll receive a password reset link. Click the link and create a new password.

**Q: I didn't receive my login credentials. What should I do?**

A: Contact the school registrar's office by phone or in person. They can verify your email address and resend your credentials or create a new account if needed.

**Q: Can I access the portal on my phone?**

A: Yes! The portal works on any device with a web browser. For best experience, use the latest version of Chrome, Safari, Firefox, or Edge.

**Q: I have multiple children at the school. Do I need separate accounts?**

A: No. One parent account can be linked to all your children at Southville 8B NHS. You can switch between children using the dropdown menu on your dashboard.

**Q: Can both parents have separate accounts?**

A: Yes. Both parents/guardians can have their own accounts to access the same child's information. Contact the school to set up a second parent account.

### Grades and Academic Performance

**Q: When are grades posted?**

A: Teachers update grades throughout the quarter. Individual assignment grades are posted within 1-2 weeks of submission. Official quarterly grades are posted at the end of each grading period.

**Q: My child says they submitted an assignment, but it shows as missing. Why?**

A: Several possibilities: 1) Assignment was submitted late, 2) Technical issue with submission, 3) Teacher hasn't updated status yet. Check with your child first, then contact the teacher if needed.

**Q: What if I think a grade is incorrect?**

A: First, discuss with your child to understand the grade. If you still have concerns, politely contact the teacher through the portal messaging system. Teachers can review and correct errors.

**Q: What is a "good" grade?**

A: In the Philippine system, 90-100 is Outstanding, 85-89 is Very Satisfactory, and 80-84 is Satisfactory. However, the "best" grade is one that reflects your child's full effort and shows improvement over time.

**Q: How often should I check grades?**

A: We recommend checking once a week. This keeps you informed without being overwhelming. Check more frequently if your child is struggling or during exam periods.

### Attendance

**Q: I received an absence notification, but my child was at school. Why?**

A: This occasionally happens due to: 1) Late arrival after attendance was taken, 2) Attendance recording error, 3) Child was in clinic/office during attendance. Contact the school office to verify and correct.

**Q: How do I submit an excuse letter for an absence?**

A: Log into the portal, go to Attendance > Report Absence, fill out the form, and upload any supporting documents (like a medical certificate). Submit within 3 days of the absence.

**Q: How many absences are too many?**

A: DepEd recommends at least 95% attendance. Missing more than 10% of school days (approximately 18 days per year) is considered chronic absenteeism and can affect academic performance.

**Q: My child was late because of traffic. Will this be excused?**

A: Traffic is generally not considered an excused tardiness. Try to leave earlier to account for traffic. However, if there was an extraordinary traffic situation, explain this to the school.

### Communication

**Q: How quickly will teachers respond to my messages?**

A: Teachers typically respond within 1-2 school days. They may not respond during weekends, holidays, or after school hours. For urgent matters, call the school office.

**Q: Can I email teachers directly instead of using the portal?**

A: The school prefers portal messaging because: 1) It's documented, 2) Teachers can access it easily, 3) Messages are archived for reference. However, teachers may provide email for urgent matters.

**Q: I want to meet with a teacher. How do I schedule a conference?**

A: Use the portal to request a conference (Messages > Request Conference), contact the teacher directly through messaging, or call the school office to arrange a meeting.

**Q: What if a teacher doesn't respond to my message?**

A: If you don't receive a response within 3 school days: 1) Send a polite follow-up message, 2) Contact the school office, 3) Request assistance from the principal.

### Technical Issues

**Q: The portal isn't loading. What should I do?**

A: Try these steps: 1) Check your internet connection, 2) Clear your browser cache, 3) Try a different browser, 4) Restart your device. If still not working, contact the school IT office.

**Q: I'm getting an error message. What does it mean?**

A: Take a screenshot of the error message and send it to the school IT support. Common errors often relate to browser compatibility or session timeouts. Try logging out and back in.

**Q: Why can't I see my child's grades?**

A: Possible reasons: 1) Grades haven't been posted yet, 2) You're looking at wrong quarter, 3) Technical issue, 4) Account permission problem. Contact the school registrar if problem persists.

**Q: Can I print information from the portal?**

A: Yes. Most pages have a "Print" option, or you can use your browser's print function (usually Ctrl+P or Cmd+P). For best results, use "Print Preview" first.

### Report Cards and Documents

**Q: When are report cards released?**

A: Report cards are released approximately 1-2 weeks after the end of each quarter. Check the school calendar for specific distribution dates.

**Q: Can I download my child's report card?**

A: Yes. Go to Report Cards section, select the quarter, and click "Download" or "Print." Save the PDF for your records.

**Q: Do I still need to get the physical report card?**

A: Yes, if the school distributes physical report cards. You typically need to claim it, sign it, and return it to the school. The digital copy is for your reference.

**Q: How do I get my child's transcript or certificates?**

A: Transcripts and official certificates are requested through the school registrar's office, not through the portal. Contact the registrar for the request process.

### General

**Q: Is my information secure on the portal?**

A: Yes. The portal uses encryption and security measures to protect your information. Always keep your password private and log out when using shared computers.

**Q: Who can see my child's information?**

A: Only parents/guardians linked to the account, the student, their teachers, and authorized school personnel can view student information. Data is protected by privacy laws.

**Q: Can my child see what I see in the portal?**

A: Students have their own portal with similar information (grades, assignments, calendar). They cannot see your parent portal or your messages with teachers.

**Q: What if I notice incorrect information about my child?**

A: Contact the school registrar immediately to correct personal information (name, address, etc.). For grade errors, contact the specific teacher.

**Q: The school year ended. Can I still access last year's information?**

A: Yes. You can typically access archived information from previous school years. Use the school year selector to view past data.
