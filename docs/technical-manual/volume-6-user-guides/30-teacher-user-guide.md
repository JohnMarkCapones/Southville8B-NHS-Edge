## Introduction

Welcome to the Southville 8B NHS Edge Teacher Portal! This comprehensive guide will help you effectively use the digital platform to manage your classes, create engaging content, assess student performance, and communicate with students and parents.

### What You'll Learn

By the end of this guide, you will be able to:
- Navigate the teacher dashboard efficiently
- Manage classes and student rosters
- Create and distribute assignments and quizzes
- Grade student work and provide meaningful feedback
- Upload and organize learning modules
- Generate comprehensive reports and analytics
- Communicate effectively with students and parents
- Track attendance and student engagement
- Use advanced features to enhance teaching effectiveness

### System Requirements

To use the teacher portal effectively, you need:
- **Internet Connection**: Stable broadband (minimum 5 Mbps recommended)
- **Web Browser**: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge (latest versions)
- **Device**: Desktop computer or laptop (tablet for basic functions)
- **Screen Resolution**: Minimum 1280x768 (larger recommended for grading)
- **Document Software**: Microsoft Office or compatible for creating materials

📝 **Note**: While the portal is mobile-responsive, many teacher functions are optimized for desktop use, especially grading and content creation.

## Getting Started

### First-Time Login

Your teacher account is created by the school administration. Follow these steps to access your account:

1. **Navigate to the portal**:
   ```
   https://southville8b-nhs-edge.edu.ph/teacher
   ```

2. **Click "Teacher Login"** or select "Teacher" from the role dropdown.

   ![Teacher Login](../images/teacher-login-page.png)

3. **Enter your credentials**:
   - **Email**: Your school email (format: `lastname.firstname@southville8b.edu.ph`)
   - **Password**: Temporary password provided by administration

4. **Complete first-time setup**:
   - Change your password (required on first login)
   - Verify your email address
   - Complete your profile information
   - Review and accept the teacher code of conduct

   ![First Login Setup](../images/teacher-first-login-setup.png)

5. **Set up two-factor authentication** (recommended):
   - Choose authentication method (SMS or authenticator app)
   - Follow the setup wizard
   - Save backup codes in a secure location

✅ **Success**: You're now logged into your teacher portal!

**Quick Action Panel** (right side, collapsible):

## Teacher Dashboard Overview

Your dashboard provides a comprehensive view of your teaching activities and student progress.

### Dashboard Components

#### 1. Welcome and Quick Stats

Displays:
- Personalized greeting with current date/time
- Total classes taught
- Total students across all classes
- Pending items requiring attention
- Today's schedule overview

![Dashboard Stats](../images/teacher-dashboard-stats.png)

#### 2. Today's Schedule

Shows your daily class schedule:
- Subject and section
- Time and room location
- Number of students
- Class status (upcoming, in progress, completed)
- Quick links to attendance and class page

#### 3. Pending Actions

Alerts you to items needing attention:
- **Submissions to Grade**: Number of ungraded assignments and quizzes
- **Missing Grades**: Students without grades for the grading period
- **Messages**: Unread messages from students and parents
- **Attendance**: Classes without recorded attendance
- **Scheduled Quizzes**: Quizzes starting soon or currently active

![Pending Actions](../images/teacher-pending-actions.png)

💡 **Tip**: Click on any pending action to go directly to that task.

#### 4. Recent Activity

Timeline of recent student activities:
- Assignment submissions
- Quiz completions
- Student questions or messages
- Grade changes or disputes
- Timestamp and student name for each activity

#### 5. Class Performance Overview

Visual analytics showing:
- Average class performance by subject
- Grade distribution charts
- Assignment completion rates
- Quiz score trends
- Comparison across sections (if teaching multiple)

![Performance Overview](../images/teacher-performance-charts.png)

#### 6. Upcoming Deadlines

Your created assignments and quizzes with approaching deadlines:
- Assignment/quiz name
- Section(s) assigned to
- Due date and time
- Submission count vs. total students
- Quick access to view submissions

#### 7. Announcements and Reminders

- School-wide announcements
- Department updates
- Personal reminders you've set
- System notifications about platform updates

## Managing Your Profile

Your teacher profile contains professional information visible to students and parents.

### Accessing Your Profile

1. Click your **profile picture/name** in the top-right
2. Select **"My Profile"** from the dropdown

### Profile Information

Your profile displays:

**Basic Information**:
- Full name and employee ID
- Profile picture
- Department and position
- Subjects taught
- Contact information

**Professional Information**:
- Educational background
- Certifications and specializations
- Years of experience
- Professional bio

**Contact and Availability**:
- School email and extension
- Office hours
- Preferred communication method
- Response time expectations

![Teacher Profile](../images/teacher-profile-view.png)

### Editing Your Profile

Update your professional information:

1. Click **"Edit Profile"** button
2. Update editable fields:
   - Profile picture
   - Bio/About me (max 500 words)
   - Office hours
   - Contact preferences
   - Educational background (if not locked by admin)
   - Professional interests
3. Upload credentials or certificates (optional)
4. Click **"Save Changes"**

#### Profile Picture Guidelines

Professional profile pictures help students identify you:

**Requirements**:
- Professional headshot
- Clear, well-lit photo
- Appropriate attire
- Neutral background preferred
- JPG or PNG format
- Maximum 5MB
- Minimum 400x400 pixels

**To Update**:
1. Click current profile picture
2. Upload new image
3. Crop and adjust
4. Click **"Save"**

### Office Hours Management

Set and communicate your availability:

1. Go to **Profile → Office Hours**
2. Click **"Add Office Hours"**
3. Set:
   - Days of the week
   - Time slots
   - Location (classroom, faculty room, online)
   - Meeting type (drop-in, appointment only)
4. Enable/disable booking system
5. Click **"Save Schedule"**

![Office Hours](../images/teacher-office-hours.png)

Students can:
- View your office hours on your profile
- Book appointments (if enabled)
- Receive confirmation and reminders

### Privacy and Visibility Settings

Control what information is visible:

1. Go to **Profile → Settings → Privacy**
2. Configure visibility:
   - **Profile Visibility**: Students, parents, staff, or public
   - **Contact Information**: Full, partial, or restricted
   - **Office Hours**: Public or by appointment only
   - **Performance Data**: Show/hide class statistics on profile
3. Click **"Save Settings"**

### Notification Preferences

Customize how you receive notifications:

1. Go to **Profile → Settings → Notifications**
2. Set preferences for each notification type:

**Submission Notifications**:
- New assignment submissions: Immediate, digest, or off
- Quiz completions: Immediate, digest, or off
- Delivery method: In-portal, email, both

**Student Communication**:
- Student messages: Immediate or digest
- Parent messages: Immediate or digest
- Question forum posts: Immediate, daily digest, or off

**Grading Reminders**:
- Ungraded submissions: Daily reminder or off
- Grade deadline approaching: 3 days, 1 day, or off

**System Notifications**:
- Platform updates: Email only
- Schedule changes: Immediate
- School announcements: As posted

3. Set quiet hours (no notifications during specified times)
4. Click **"Save Preferences"**

![Notification Settings](../images/teacher-notification-prefs.png)

💡 **Tip**: Use daily digest mode during teaching hours to minimize interruptions, then switch to immediate for office hours.

![Class Overview](../images/teacher-class-overview.png)

### Class Settings

Configure class-specific settings:

1. Click **"Class Settings"** (gear icon)
2. Configure:

**General Settings**:
- Class description
- Syllabus upload
- Class banner image
- Public visibility (parents can view)

**Grading Settings**:
- Grading scale
- Component weights (quizzes, assignments, exams, participation)
- Late submission policy
- Extra credit options

**Student Settings**:
- Allow student-to-student messaging

![Class Settings](../images/teacher-class-settings.png)

### Archiving Classes

At the end of a term or year:

1. Open the class
2. Click **"Class Settings"**
3. Select **"Archive Class"**
4. Confirm archiving

**What happens when you archive**:
- Class becomes read-only
- Students can view but not submit new work
- You can view and export all data
- Class removed from active classes list
- Accessible from "Archived Classes" section

📝 **Note**: Only archive classes at the end of grading periods. Archived classes cannot be unarchived without administrator assistance.

Manage your student roster and individual student information.

1. Open a class
2. Click the **"Students"** tab
3. View complete student list

![Student Roster](../images/teacher-student-roster.png)

**Student List Information**:
- Student name and ID
- Profile picture
- Current grade in class
- Attendance rate
- Submission status
- Last active date
- Quick action buttons

### Sorting and Filtering Students

**Sort Options**:
- Alphabetically (A-Z or Z-A)
- By current grade (highest/lowest)
- By attendance rate
- By submission count
- By last active date

**Filter Options**:
- Performance level (struggling, average, excelling)
- Attendance status (regular, concerning, critical)
- Submission status (up-to-date, behind, significantly behind)
- Active/inactive

### Viewing Individual Student Profiles

Click on a student name to view their detailed profile:

**Student Information**:
- Basic details (name, ID, contact)
- Parent/guardian information
- Academic history
- Special needs or accommodations

**Performance in Your Class**:
- Current grade breakdown
- Assignment completion list
- Quiz scores
- Attendance record
- Participation notes

**Communication History**:
- Message thread
- Parent communications
- Notes and observations

![Student Profile](../images/teacher-student-detail.png)

### Adding Notes to Student Profiles

Document observations and interventions:

1. Open student profile
2. Click **"Add Note"** button
3. Select note type:
   - Academic observation
   - Behavioral note
   - Intervention record
   - Positive recognition
   - Parent contact log
4. Enter note details
5. Set visibility (private to you, share with advisers, share with parents)
6. Click **"Save Note"**

**Note Best Practices**:
- Be objective and factual
- Include dates and specific examples
- Document interventions and their outcomes
- Note improvements and achievements
- Maintain professional language

### Tracking Student Accommodations

For students with special needs:

1. View student profile
2. Check **"Accommodations"** section
3. Review documented accommodations:
   - Extended time on tests
   - Modified assignments
   - Assistive technology
   - Preferential seating
   - Other IEP/504 accommodations

4. Apply accommodations when creating assignments/quizzes

📝 **Note**: Accommodation information is set by administrators and counselors. Contact the guidance office if updates are needed.

### Monitoring At-Risk Students

Identify and support struggling students:

1. Go to **Students → At-Risk Report**
2. View flagged students based on:
   - Failing grades (below 75%)
   - Poor attendance (below 80%)
   - Missing assignments (3+ overdue)
   - Declining performance trend
   - No recent activity (7+ days)

3. Click on student to view details
4. Document intervention steps
5. Contact student and/or parents

![At-Risk Students](../images/teacher-at-risk-students.png)

### Exporting Student Data

Export student information for records or analysis:

1. Go to **Students** tab in a class
2. Click **"Export"** button
3. Select export format:
   - Excel (.xlsx)
   - CSV
   - PDF report
4. Choose data to include:
   - Basic information
   - Grades
   - Attendance
   - Submission history
5. Click **"Download"**

💡 **Tip**: Use Excel exports for detailed grade analysis and pivot tables.

## Creating and Managing Assignments

Create, distribute, and manage assignments efficiently.

### Creating a New Assignment

1. Open a class
2. Click the **"Assignments"** tab
3. Click **"Create Assignment"** button
4. Fill in assignment details:

**Basic Information**:
- Assignment title
- Description and instructions (rich text editor)
- Subject/topic tags
- Assignment type (homework, project, essay, etc.)

**Submission Settings**:
- Submission type:
  - Text entry
  - File upload
  - Both
  - External tool
- Accepted file types (if file upload)
- Maximum file size
- Number of files allowed

**Grading**:
- Points possible
- Grading rubric (create or select existing)
- Group assignment (yes/no)

**Availability**:
- Available from (date and time)
- Due date and time
- Late submission acceptance:
  - Not accepted
  - Accepted with penalty (specify %)
  - Accepted without penalty
- Lock after due date (yes/no)

**Additional Options**:
- Attach reference files or resources
- Require peer review
- Allow resubmissions (number of attempts)
- Show to students (publish now or save as draft)

5. Click **"Create Assignment"** or **"Save as Draft"**

![Create Assignment](../images/teacher-create-assignment.png)

### Using the Rich Text Editor

The assignment editor provides formatting tools:

**Text Formatting**:
- Bold, italic, underline, strikethrough
- Font size and color
- Headings (H1-H6)
- Paragraph styles

**Lists and Alignment**:
- Bullet points and numbered lists
- Text alignment (left, center, right, justify)
- Indentation

**Media and Links**:
- Insert images (upload or URL)
- Embed videos (YouTube, Vimeo)
- Insert links
- Insert tables

**Special Elements**:
- Mathematical equations (MathJax support)
- Code blocks (for programming classes)
- Blockquotes
- Horizontal rules

💡 **Tip**: Use headings to structure complex assignment instructions. Students can scan headings to quickly understand requirements.

### Creating Rubrics

Rubrics provide clear grading criteria:

1. In the assignment creation form, click **"Create Rubric"**
2. Add criteria:
   - Criterion name (e.g., "Content Quality")
   - Description
   - Point value
3. Add performance levels for each criterion:
   - Exemplary (4 points)
   - Proficient (3 points)
   - Developing (2 points)
   - Beginning (1 point)
4. Customize descriptions for each level
5. Click **"Save Rubric"**

![Create Rubric](../images/teacher-create-rubric.png)

**Rubric Benefits**:
- Consistent grading across students
- Clear expectations for students
- Faster grading process
- Detailed feedback aligned to criteria
- Reusable for similar assignments

### Attaching Resources

Provide reference materials with assignments:

1. In assignment creation, scroll to **"Resources"** section
2. Click **"Attach Files"**
3. Upload files from your computer or select from your library
4. Add:
   - File description
   - Whether file is required or optional
5. Organize files in logical order

**Supported File Types**:
- Documents: PDF, DOC, DOCX
- Spreadsheets: XLS, XLSX
- Presentations: PPT, PPTX
- Images: JPG, PNG, GIF
- Videos: MP4, MOV
- Compressed: ZIP

### Assigning to Multiple Sections

Assign the same assignment to multiple classes:

1. In assignment creation, check **"Assign to multiple sections"**
2. Select sections from the list
3. Set due dates:
   - Same due date for all sections
   - Different due dates per section
4. Customize instructions per section (optional)
5. Click **"Create Assignment"**

📝 **Note**: Multi-section assignments create separate assignment instances per section but share the same content and rubric.

### Managing Existing Assignments

View and manage all assignments:

1. Go to **Assignments** tab
2. View assignments by status:
   - **Active**: Currently accepting submissions
   - **Upcoming**: Not yet available to students
   - **Closed**: Past due date
   - **Draft**: Not published

**Assignment Actions**:
- **Edit**: Modify assignment details (before due date)
- **Duplicate**: Create a copy for another section or term
- **Extend Deadline**: Give more time for specific students or entire class
- **Delete**: Remove assignment (only if no submissions)
- **View Submissions**: See student work
- **Download All**: Bulk download submissions

![Manage Assignments](../images/teacher-manage-assignments.png)

### Editing Published Assignments

Modify assignments after publishing:

1. Open the assignment
2. Click **"Edit Assignment"**
3. Make changes

⚠️ **Warning**: Some changes have restrictions:
- **Can change**: Instructions, due date, points possible
- **Cannot change**: Submission type (if submissions exist)
- **Caution**: Changing points requires re-grading

**Students are notified** when you update:
- Assignment instructions
- Due dates
- Grading criteria

### Extending Deadlines

Grant extensions to individual students or entire class:

**For Individual Students**:
1. Open assignment
2. Click **"Submissions"** tab
3. Find student
4. Click **"Actions"** → **"Grant Extension"**
5. Set new due date
6. Add reason (optional)
7. Click **"Grant"**

**For Entire Class**:
1. Open assignment
2. Click **"Edit Assignment"**
3. Change due date
4. Check **"Apply to all students, even those with individual extensions"** if needed
5. Click **"Save"**

### Duplicating Assignments

Reuse assignments for other sections or terms:

1. Find assignment to duplicate
2. Click **"Actions"** → **"Duplicate"**
3. Modify:
   - Assignment name (add section or term identifier)
   - Due dates
   - Assigned sections
4. Click **"Create Duplicate"**

💡 **Tip**: Create a template assignment with your standard instructions and rubrics, then duplicate and customize for each unit.

## Quiz Builder and Management

Create comprehensive assessments using the built-in quiz builder.

### Creating a New Quiz

1. Open a class
2. Go to **Quizzes** tab
3. Click **"Create Quiz"** button
4. Configure quiz settings:

**Basic Information**:
- Quiz title
- Description and instructions
- Category/topic tags

**Timing**:
- Time limit (minutes) or unlimited
- Available from (date and time)
- Due date and time
- Show results immediately or after manual release

**Question Settings**:
- Questions per page (one or multiple)
- Randomize question order
- Randomize answer choices
- Show one question at a time

**Attempts**:
- Number of attempts allowed (1-10 or unlimited)
- Scoring method if multiple attempts:
  - Highest score
  - Average score
  - Last attempt
  - First attempt

**Access**:
- Require password (optional)
- Restrict to IP range (for in-class quizzes)
- Browser lockdown (prevent tab switching)

![Quiz Settings](../images/teacher-quiz-settings.png)

5. Click **"Save and Add Questions"**

### Adding Questions

The quiz builder supports multiple question types:

#### 1. Multiple Choice

1. Click **"Add Question"** → **"Multiple Choice"**
2. Enter question text (supports rich text and images)
3. Add answer options (minimum 2)
4. Select the correct answer(s)
5. Set points value
6. Add feedback for correct/incorrect answers (optional)
7. Click **"Save Question"**

![Multiple Choice Question](../images/teacher-quiz-mc-question.png)

**Multiple Choice Options**:
- Single answer (radio buttons)
- Multiple answers (checkboxes)
- Partial credit for multiple-answer questions
- Randomize answer order

#### 2. True/False

1. Click **"Add Question"** → **"True/False"**
2. Enter question statement
3. Select correct answer (True or False)
4. Set points value
5. Add feedback (optional)
6. Click **"Save Question"**

#### 3. Short Answer

1. Click **"Add Question"** → **"Short Answer"**
2. Enter question
3. Provide model answer(s) or keywords
4. Set points value
5. Choose grading:
   - Auto-grade (exact match)
   - Manual grading required
6. Click **"Save Question"**

#### 4. Essay/Long Answer

1. Click **"Add Question"** → **"Essay"**
2. Enter question and instructions
3. Set points value
4. Set minimum/maximum word count (optional)
5. Provide grading rubric
6. Click **"Save Question"**

📝 **Note**: Essay questions always require manual grading.

#### 5. Matching

1. Click **"Add Question"** → **"Matching"**
2. Enter question prompt
3. Add matching pairs:
   - Left column items
   - Right column matches
4. Add distractors (extra right column items)
5. Set points (per correct match or total)
6. Click **"Save Question"**

#### 6. Fill in the Blank

1. Click **"Add Question"** → **"Fill in Blank"**
2. Enter sentence with [BLANK] placeholders
3. Provide correct answer(s) for each blank
4. Allow spelling variations (optional)
5. Set points per blank
6. Click **"Save Question"**

### Question Bank

Build a reusable question library:

**Creating Question Bank**:
1. Go to **Quizzes → Question Bank**
2. Click **"Create Bank"**
3. Name the bank (e.g., "Math Chapter 5 Questions")
4. Add questions to the bank
5. Organize with tags and categories

**Using Question Bank**:
1. When creating a quiz, click **"Add from Bank"**
2. Browse or search your question banks
3. Select questions to add
4. Questions are copied (not linked) to the quiz

💡 **Tip**: Build comprehensive question banks throughout the semester. You can generate randomized quizzes from banks for reviews and makeup tests.

### Randomizing Quizzes

Create unique quiz versions for each student:

1. In quiz settings, enable **"Randomize Questions"**
2. Choose randomization method:
   - **Random from bank**: Pull N questions randomly from question bank
   - **Random order**: Show all questions in random order
   - **Random sections**: Mix question groups
3. Enable **"Randomize answer choices"** for multiple-choice questions
4. Set different question versions (if using bank)

**Benefits of Randomization**:
- Reduces cheating
- Tests understanding, not memorization
- Fair assessment across attempts

### Importing Questions

Bulk import questions from files:

1. Click **"Import Questions"**
2. Choose import format:
   - QTI (Question and Test Interoperability)
   - Aiken format
   - CSV
   - Word document (formatted)
3. Upload file
4. Map fields (for CSV)
5. Preview imported questions
6. Click **"Import"**

![Import Questions](../images/teacher-import-questions.png)

### Managing Quiz Questions

After adding questions:

**Reorder Questions**:
- Drag and drop questions to reorder
- Group related questions together

**Edit Questions**:
- Click on a question to edit
- Changes apply immediately (if quiz not started)

**Delete Questions**:
- Click delete icon on question
- Confirm deletion

⚠️ **Warning**: Cannot delete or significantly modify questions after students have started taking the quiz. Points can still be adjusted.

### Publishing Quizzes

Control when students can access quizzes:

**Save as Draft**:
- Quiz is created but not visible to students
- Continue editing
- Publish when ready

**Publish Immediately**:
- Quiz becomes visible to students
- Available based on your date/time settings
- Students receive notification

**Schedule Publishing**:
- Set specific date/time for quiz to become available
- Automatic notification sent to students

### Monitoring Active Quizzes

View quiz progress in real-time:

1. Open quiz
2. Click **"Monitor Attempts"**
3. View:
   - Students currently taking quiz
   - Time remaining per student
   - Questions answered
   - Progress percentage

![Monitor Quiz](../images/teacher-monitor-quiz.png)

**Live Monitoring Actions**:
- Grant time extension to individual student
- View student's answers in real-time (without interfering)
- Send message to student taking quiz
- End quiz for specific student (emergencies)

### Quiz Security Features

Prevent cheating and ensure integrity:

**Browser Lockdown**:
- Prevents tab switching
- Disables right-click and copy
- Requires full-screen mode
- Detects attempts to leave quiz

**Proctoring Options**:
- Require webcam (if implemented)
- Take periodic screenshots
- Log suspicious activities

**Access Control**:
- IP restriction (for in-class quizzes)
- Password protection
- One-time access codes

📝 **Note**: Balance security with accessibility. Too many restrictions may cause technical issues for legitimate students.

## Grading and Feedback

Efficiently grade student work and provide meaningful feedback.

### Accessing Submissions

View student submissions:

**From Dashboard**:
- Click on pending grading count
- Takes you to grading queue

**From Assignment**:
1. Open assignment
2. Click **"Submissions"** tab

### Grade Book Views

Switch between different views:

**Spreadsheet View**:
- Traditional grid layout
- All grades visible at once

**Student View**:
- Focus on one student at a time

**Standards View** (if using standards-based grading):

### Adding Grade Categories

Organize grades into weighted categories:

1. Click **"Grade Categories"**
2. Add category:
   - Category name (e.g., "Quizzes", "Projects")
   - Weight percentage (e.g., 30%)
   - Drop lowest N grades (optional)
3. Assign existing grades to categories
4. Click **"Save Categories"**

**Example Grading Scheme**:
- Quizzes: 30%
- Assignments: 25%
- Projects: 20%
- Midterm Exam: 10%
- Final Exam: 15%
- Total: 100%

### Manual Grade Entry

Add grades not from assignments/quizzes:

1. Click **"Add Grade Column"**
2. Enter:
   - Item name (e.g., "Class Participation", "Lab Practical")
   - Points possible
   - Category
   - Date

4. Press Tab to move to next student
5. Changes save automatically

### Grade Calculations

**Automatic Calculations**:
The system automatically calculates:
- Category averages
- Weighted total grade
- Letter grade (based on scale)
- GWA contribution
- Class average
- Grade percentile/rank (if enabled)

**Calculation Methods**:
- **Points-based**: Total points earned / total points possible
- **Weighted categories**: Each category contributes its weight percentage
- **Standards-based**: Mastery level across learning standards

### Handling Missing Grades

Configure how missing grades affect calculations:

1. Go to **Gradebook Settings**
2. Set missing grade policy:
   - **Count as zero**: Missing = 0 points (affects average)
   - **Exclude from calculation**: Only grade completed work
   - **Set minimum**: Missing can't go below certain % (e.g., 50%)
3. Mark incomplete work as:
   - Missing (M)
   - Incomplete (I)
   - Excused (E)

💡 **Tip**: Excluding missing grades from calculation provides a more accurate picture of student mastery but may hide work habits.

### Grade Comments

   - All students
   - Selected students
   - All columns or selected columns

**Use Cases**:
- Submit grades to registrar

### Grade Posting Settings

Control grade visibility:

**Posting Settings**:
1. Go to **Gradebook → Settings → Posting**
2. Configure:
   - **Auto-post grades**: Visible to students immediately after grading
   - **Manual post**: You control when grades are released
   - **Hide final grade**: Students see individual items but not calculated total
   - **Hide grade until**: Set future date for release

**Item-Specific Posting**:
- Hide/show individual assignments
- Useful for hiding old grades during new grading period

### Grade History and Audit Trail

View all grade changes:

1. Click on a grade
2. Click **"View History"**
3. See:
   - Original grade
   - All changes with dates
   - Who made the change (you or admin)
   - Reason for change (if documented)

**Audit Trail Benefits**:
- Transparency in grade disputes
- Track grading corrections
- Accountability
- Historical record

### Curving Grades

Apply a curve to grades:

1. Select assignment column
2. Click **"Curve Grades"**
3. Choose curve method:
   - **Linear curve**: Add/subtract points to all grades
   - **Scale to target**: Scale to specific average (e.g., 85%)
   - **Bell curve**: Distribute grades in normal distribution
   - **Square root curve**: √(original_score × 100)
4. Preview results
5. Click **"Apply Curve"**

⚠️ **Warning**: Curving affects all students. Document your curving policy clearly.

Record and monitor student attendance.

### Taking Attendance

**During Class**:
1. Open class in session
2. Click **"Take Attendance"**
3. Mark each student:
   - ✅ Present
   - ❌ Absent
   - 🕒 Tardy
   - 📝 Excused absence
4. Add notes for absences/tardies if needed
5. Click **"Submit Attendance"**

![Take Attendance](../images/teacher-take-attendance.png)

**Quick Attendance**:
- Default: All present
- Click only students who are absent/tardy
- Faster for large classes

### Attendance Methods

**Manual Entry**: Click each student status

**Seating Chart**: Visual grid layout, click seats

**Card Swipe** (if hardware available): Students swipe ID cards

**Voice Roll Call**: Call names, mark as you go

### Modifying Attendance

Correct errors in submitted attendance:

1. Go to **Attendance History**
2. Find the date
3. Click **"Edit"**
4. Change student status
5. Add reason for modification
6. Click **"Update"**

📝 **Note**: Attendance modifications are logged. Some schools require administrator approval for changes.

### Attendance Reports

**Class Attendance Summary**:
- Overall attendance rate
- Students with concerning attendance
- Patterns (e.g., frequent Monday absences)

**Individual Student Attendance**:
- View from student profile
- All absences and tardies
- Excused vs. unexcused

**Export Attendance**:
- Download attendance records
- Excel or PDF format
- For administration or records

### Attendance Alerts

Set up automatic alerts:

1. Go to **Attendance → Alert Settings**
2. Create alert rules:
   - X consecutive absences
   - X absences in Y days
   - Tardy count threshold
3. Choose recipients (you, parents, counselor)
4. Alerts trigger automatically

### Integration with Grading

If attendance affects grades:

1. Go to **Gradebook → Settings**
2. Enable **"Attendance Grade"**
3. Set:
   - Points for perfect attendance
   - Deduction per absence
   - Tardy policy (e.g., 3 tardies = 1 absence)
4. Attendance grade calculated automatically

## Troubleshooting

### Login Issues

**Can't Log In**:
- Verify correct email and password
- Check Caps Lock is off
- Use "Forgot Password" to reset
- Contact IT if account is locked

**Two-Factor Authentication Issues**:
- Try backup codes
- Resend authentication code
- Check device time is correct
- Contact IT to reset 2FA

### Grading Issues

**Grades Not Calculating Correctly**:
- Check category weights add to 100%
- Verify point values are correct
- Check for missing grade handling setting
- Refresh page and recalculate

**Can't Submit Grade**:
- Check internet connection
- Verify grade is within valid range
- Ensure all required rubric criteria are scored
- Try different browser

**Student Can't See Posted Grade**:
- Verify grade is marked "Return to Student"
- Check student hasn't been excluded from assignment
- Confirm assignment is published
- Check class settings for grade visibility

### Upload Issues

**Module Upload Fails**:
- Check file size (must be under limit)
- Verify file type is supported
- Try compressing file
- Check internet connection
- Upload during off-peak hours

**Large File Upload Timeout**:
- Split into smaller files
- Use video hosting for large videos
- Compress files before uploading
- Try uploading via wired connection

### Assignment Issues

**Students Can't Submit**:
- Check assignment is published and available
- Verify due date hasn't passed (if locked)
- Check file type restrictions
- Verify students have permission

**Assignment Not Appearing for Students**:
- Verify assignment is published
- Check availability dates
- Confirm assigned to correct section
- Check student enrollment in class

### Quiz Issues

**Quiz Won't Start**:
- Check availability dates

- Try different browser

**Quiz Questions Not Displaying**:
- Check question has content

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: Can I access the portal from home?**
A: Yes, the portal is accessible from anywhere with internet. Use the same login credentials.

**Q: Can I use the portal on my tablet or phone?**
A: Yes, but many teacher functions are optimized for desktop. Grading and content creation work best on larger screens.

**Q: How often should I check the portal?**
A: Daily during the school week to monitor submissions, messages, and pending items.

**Q: Can I customize my dashboard?**
A: Yes, you can toggle widgets on/off and rearrange them to suit your workflow.

### Assignment Questions

**Q: Can I edit an assignment after students have submitted?**
A: You can modify instructions and due dates, but cannot change submission types if submissions exist.

**Q: How do I handle students who submitted the wrong file?**
A: If resubmissions are allowed, have them resubmit. If not, they can send the correct file via message and you can manually replace it.

**Q: Can I reuse assignments from previous years?**
A: Yes, duplicate assignments from archived classes or save them to your assignment library.

**Q: What if an assignment deadline falls on a holiday?**
A: Change the due date. The system doesn't automatically adjust for holidays.

### Grading Questions

**Q: How long do I have to grade assignments?**
A: School policy typically requires grading within 5-7 school days. Check with your department.

**Q: Can I change a grade after releasing it to students?**
A: Yes, using grade override. Changes are logged and students are notified.

**Q: Do I have to grade every assignment?**
A: Only graded assignments count toward final grade. You can create ungraded practice assignments.

**Q: Can I drop the lowest grade from a category?**
A: Yes, configure this in grade category settings (e.g., drop lowest 2 quizzes).

### Quiz Questions

**Q: Can students see correct answers after taking a quiz?**
A: Only if you enable this in quiz settings. You control what feedback students see.

**Q: What happens if a student's computer crashes during a quiz?**
A: Answers are saved automatically. Student can log back in and continue (grant time extension if needed).

**Q: Can I see quiz results before students?**
A: Yes, you see all results immediately. Students only see results when you release them.

**Q: Can I allow students to retake a quiz?**
A: Yes, set number of attempts in quiz settings. Choose how to calculate final score (highest, average, etc.).

### Module Questions

**Q: What's the maximum file size for uploads?**
A: 100MB for standard files, 500MB for videos. Larger files should be hosted externally and linked.

**Q: Can I see who downloaded a module?**
A: Yes, click on the module and view analytics for download history.

**Q: Can I share modules across multiple classes?**
A: Yes, upload to your library then add from library to multiple classes.

**Q: What if I upload the wrong version of a file?**
A: Edit the module and replace the file. Students who downloaded the old version are notified.

### Communication Questions

**Q: Do I have to respond to all student messages?**
A: Respond to academic questions and important matters. Set expectations for response time (e.g., within 24 hours on school days).

**Q: Can parents message me directly?**
A: Yes, if they have portal access. You can also initiate contact with parents.

**Q: Are my messages private?**
A: Messages are private between participants, but administrators can access them if needed for safety or policy reasons.

**Q: How do I handle inappropriate messages?**
A: Report to administration immediately. Do not engage or respond.

### Attendance Questions

**Q: Can I change attendance after submitting?**
A: Yes, edit attendance records. Changes are logged.

**Q: What if I forgot to take attendance?**
A: Enter it as soon as you remember. Add a note explaining the late entry.

**Q: How do excused absences work?**
A: Mark as excused when student provides valid excuse (medical, family emergency, etc.). Check school policy for what qualifies.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Which browser works best?**
A: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge all work well. Keep your browser updated.

**Q: Why is the portal slow?**
A: Could be internet speed, peak usage times, or browser cache. Try clearing cache or using during off-peak hours.

**Q: Can I use the portal offline?**
A: No, you need internet connection for all teacher functions.

**Q: How do I report a bug?**
A: Contact IT support with detailed description and screenshots.

### Support Questions

**Q: Is there training available?**
A: Yes, check with the IT department for training sessions and video tutorials.

**Q: Where can I find detailed documentation?**
A: This user guide is your primary resource. Additional guides available in the Help Center.

**Q: Who do I contact for grade posting issues?**
A: IT support for technical issues, registrar for policy questions.
